## The one-line strategy

**Stop trying to make it non-technical, and make the *methodology* the product.** Sell an opinionated
"senior-AI-engineering operating system for Claude Code" to **developers, indie hackers, and small agencies**
— BYO-key — and build the durable business on the method + community, not the forkable files.

## Why (the settled findings)

1. **Not usable by non-technical people as-shipped.** The git/terminal/CLI + BYO-Anthropic-subscription
   on-ramp is a hard wall. Every "anyone can build" wave (Visual Basic, FrontPage, WordPress, Bubble) landed
   on *semi-technical power-users*, never civilians. No lens defended non-technical direct use.
2. **Legally licensable today.** obra/superpowers is MIT (verified); BYO-subscription is the Anthropic-ToS-clean
   path (routing requests through *others'* credentials is prohibited). But Anthropic re-drew the third-party
   line 3× in 2026 (Feb ban → Apr enforcement → May metering **paused** June 15) → treat policy as a moving
   target and publish a BYO-key notice.
3. **The files have no moat.** SKILL.md is an open cross-tool standard; hundreds of free skills exist; the
   platform can absorb the capability (cf. Apple Sherlock, Twitter API, Anthropic's own browser "Projects").
4. **The durable, proven buyer is the developer / agency / "Claude Code consultant"** — one abstraction above
   the platform — not the civilian.

## The plan — three horizons

### Horizon 1 — Revenue in ~2 weeks (proven, low-risk)
- **Reframe the positioning (free, do first):** "an opinionated senior-AI-engineering OS for Claude Code."
  Drop "non-tech-savvy / welcome everyone" from the pitch; lead with "save 3 months," the roast/vetting gate,
  and the 6-rung path-to-production discipline.
- **Package as a premium paid foundation:** **$199–$399 one-time** (comps: Makerkit/Supastarter $299–599,
  ShipFast $199 base). Priced at the top of the band because it's a whole *methodology*, not just auth+billing.
- **Add an Agency / white-label tier: $499–$999** (rebrand rights for client work). This is the ThemeForest
  "arm the professionals" play — the best-paying, stickiest buyer.
- **Distribution (all at once):** own landing page + Gumroad/Lemon Squeezy · a paid Claude-Code-skills
  marketplace (**Agensi**, 70% creator share) · a launch push (Product Hunt, indie-hacker/X communities).
- **Compliance foundation:** add a `LICENSE` (commercial terms) + the one-line BYO-key ToS notice.

### Horizon 2 — Build the moat the files lack (months 1–3, in parallel)
- **Brand the methodology as public IP** — a named "playbook"/whitepaper of the idea→ship discipline (marketing
  *and* the non-forkable asset).
- **A recurring layer without becoming a SaaS:** paid updates / a maintenance subscription (new skills, rungs,
  providers) + a private community + a **certification** ("Certified Builder") for the consultant class. The
  `advise-project` loop already built is a native recurring hook.
- **Vertical editions:** "law-firm-site factory," "restaurant-app factory," etc. — each a premium SKU with
  pre-loaded charter/design/compliance, seeded by the same engine.

### Horizon 3 — The 100× bet (demand-gated; only after H1/H2 validate)
- **The hosted `aios/` console as a BYO-key web front door** — "sign up → describe → watch it build." The
  *only* version that reaches non-technical users, and the Lovable/Replit-tier business ($200M ARR / $6.6B).
  But months of build + real infra + a support burden for ~68%-first-run-success generated apps + head-on
  competition. Fund it from H1/H2 revenue; do not start here.

## Do THIS week (cheapest, highest-signal — before building anything)
1. **Pre-sell test:** a one-page landing site for the developer version with a "$X early access" button.
   Devs pre-pay → the market is real. (~1 day.)
2. **Honesty test:** 5 real non-technical users + only the README → count who reaches a running console
   (predict ~0; note where they stall). Kills the non-tech claim and refocuses the product.

## The frontier bet (the biggest upside no lens priced)
Maybe the winning product isn't the template at all — it's a **methodology + certification + community** that
makes this the default "operating system" the emerging class of Claude Code consultants standardizes on. The
brand + method + network is the one asset Anthropic can't Sherlock and a buyer can't fork in a weekend.

## Caveats
- The actual license **terms** + the liability disclaimers on the generated legal/payments outputs need a real
  lawyer (not counsel here).
- ShipFast revenue is self-reported; Bolt's ARR is an estimate; the TAM is one vendor's forecast; Anthropic's
  metering is announced-but-paused. (See the briefing's claim-safety guide.)
