# Roast Verdict — Margin Guard (Shopify discount-leak protection app)

**The brief:** Margin Guard — a Shopify App Store app in the "cut losses / save money" category that
protects a merchant's margin from discount leakage: (1) detects/blocks auto-apply coupon browser
extensions (Honey, Capital One Shopping, Cently) at checkout; (2) stops discount-code stacking/abuse;
(3) monitors where the merchant's codes leak to public coupon sites; plus a monthly "margin saved"
dashboard. Recurring SaaS ~$19-49/mo. Builder: solo product entrepreneur (builds to sell), <$1k budget,
no audience, relaxed timeline; distribution = Shopify App Store discovery + SEO + short-form demos.
Reached via a brainstorming pass after the founder's original idea (AI pitch-deck generator) was vetted
as poor founder-idea fit.

## THE VERDICT: RESHAPE (leaning kill-as-specified)
Confidence: medium-high

**The call in one line:** Don't build Margin-Guard-as-specified — the coupon-blocking niche is already
owned by a 5-star incumbent, the "margin saved" number is a counterfactual you can't honestly compute,
and the core feature is a fragile browser arms-race — but the Shopify + cut-losses + marketplace-
distribution frame is correct, so pivot to a "found-money" wedge with provable ROI.

**Why:** Council split 2/8/2/3/3; the four skeptics converged on three fatal specifics, all corroborated.
(1) The exact product already ships, 5-starred: KeepCart (5.0★, 73 reviews, $39-199/mo, "works with
Checkout Extensibility"), plus Veeper, Omnipotent, Promo Pirates, Vigilance — Margin Guard's precise
spec, already branded and priced; you'd be the 5th entrant behind a perfect-rated incumbent with no
audience and <$1k. (2) "Blocked a coupon ≠ saved margin" — the hero metric is an unobservable
counterfactual (ignores abandonment = losing the whole sale, entitled codes, and the merchant's own
public codes), so the dashboard is fabricated by construction and the Buyer would smell it. (3) The
villain is cratering — Honey lost ~30% of users after the MegaLag exposé + lawsuit. The Expansionist's
bull case (governance is the retention engine) is real but already served by those same incumbents.
Correction to the brief: "technically impossible on non-Plus checkout" was OVERSTATED — KeepCart proves
it's doable on Checkout Extensibility, but as a cat-and-mouse hack (not a durable API), which is worse
for a solo builder inheriting an arms-race against funded incumbents.

**Biggest risk:** Entering a small, occupied niche behind a 5-star incumbent, on a fragile technical
hack, selling a number merchants can tell is fake.

**Biggest upside:** The LANE is right — quantified "cut-losses" Shopify apps are the self-closing-sale
category, and the founder's marketplace+SEO+short-form edge fits it. The upside survives a change of wedge.

**Money read:** As specified, first dollar is unlikely — KeepCart's $39-199 ladder + 5-star reviews bury
a no-audience 5th entrant. The reshape (provable found-money) is where the ROI pitch becomes honest;
the strongest version (shipping-refund recovery) carries a carrier-integration build lift. Validate before
building either way.

**The cheapest 48-hour test (2 hours, zero code):** (a) Read KeepCart/Veeper/Promo Pirates App Store
reviews — confirm the coupon niche is occupied + 5-starred (kills the as-specified build) and mine the
1-3★ reviews for the gap they're NOT filling. (b) For the reshape: pick ONE found-money wedge (shipping-
refund recovery / failed-payment recovery / app-fee & overcharge audit) and DM 15-20 Shopify merchants
(r/shopify, Shopify community): "Would you pay a cut for someone to recover $X you're provably owed?"
≥3 real yeses = a wedge with a pulse AND an honest metric.

**If RESHAPE:** Keep Shopify + cut-losses + the distribution edge. Drop coupon-blocking (occupied + fake
metric + browser arms-race). Pivot to a wedge where the money is real, observable, and on the merchant's
side of the layer — recovered shipping refunds (carriers owe merchants for late/lost deliveries),
recovered failed/declined payments, or an app-fee/overcharge audit. "Found money with a receipt" beats
"blocked money you can't measure": provable ROI, no counterfactual, no shopper-browser arms-race, and
less occupied than the Honey corner.

**Council scores:** Contrarian 2/10 · Expansionist 8/10 · Logician 2/10 · Researcher 3/10 · Buyer 3/10

## Note — this is an idea-selection miss, NOT a founder-fit failure

Unlike the pitch-deck idea (which failed on the founder's distribution/edge), Margin Guard fails on the
SPECIFIC WEDGE: occupied + technically fragile + dishonest metric. The founder's strategy — Shopify App
Store + "cut losses" category + marketplace/SEO/short-form distribution — remains sound and is validated
by the Expansionist and the category logic. The fix is to pick a better wedge in the SAME lane (a
provable found-money app), not to abandon the strategy. Normal idea-selection iteration.
